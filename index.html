About Section 1

Spider-Man was co-created by Stan Lee in the early 1960s during his legendary era at Marvel Comics. Born in 1922 as Stanley Martin Lieber, Stan Lee became one of the most influential comic book writers in history. He wanted to create a teenage superhero who wasn’t a sidekick, but a relatable main character with real-life problems. His idea of a hero who struggles with school, money, and responsibility became a groundbreaking shift in the comic book world. Lee helped shape Spider-Man’s voice, personality, and the famous message: “With great power comes great responsibility.”

About Section 2

Steve Ditko

Steve Ditko was the original artist who designed Spider-Man’s iconic red-and-blue costume, web patterns, and the character’s mysterious and acrobatic style. Ditko illustrated Spider-Man’s first appearances, visually building the world of Peter Parker, his villains, and New York City.

Jack Kirby

Jack Kirby helped Stan Lee shape the early concept of Spider-Man. Although his version wasn’t used in the final design, his input helped guide the direction of the character. Kirby is known as one of Marvel’s greatest artists and played a role in developing many foundational heroes.

John Romita Sr.

John Romita Sr. became the lead artist after Steve Ditko left Marvel. He gave Spider-Man a cleaner, more expressive style and helped define the look of many supporting characters like Mary Jane Watson. His art played a major role in making Spider-Man one of Marvel’s top heroes.

The Major History Events of Spider-Man

  1. 1962 Spider-Man makes his first appearance in Amazing Fantasy #15, written by Stan Lee and illustrated by Steve Ditko.
  2. 1963 The Amazing Spider-Man #1 is released, kicking off his first solo comic series.
  3. 1967 The first Spider-Man animated TV series premieres, introducing the famous “Spider-Man, Spider-Man…” theme song.
  4. 1977 The first live-action Spider-Man TV show airs, starring Nicholas Hammond.
  5. 1984 Spider-Man gains the black “symbiote” suit for the first time in Secret Wars #8, eventually leading to the creation of Venom.
  6. 2002 The first major live-action Spider-Man movie releases, directed by Sam Raimi and starring Tobey Maguire.
  7. 2018 Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse premieres, introducing Miles Morales to a wider audience and winning the Oscar for Best Animated Feature.
  8. 2021 Spider-Man: No Way Home is released, uniting three generations of Spider-Man actors in one movie.
